gist: >
  This is the editorial introduction to a special issue of the Journal on Multimodal User
  Interfaces devoted to AR/VR/MR remote collaboration. It organizes two decades of research
  around five factors (task, local user, remote user, communication, awareness
  tool/interface) and three research threads -- multimodal communication cues (pointer,
  sketch, gesture, gaze, spatial audio), view sharing/situational awareness, and human
  factors such as co-presence, cognitive load, and trust. It previews the five accepted
  feature papers' findings, for example that combining visual cues lowers cognitive load,
  spatialized audio improves object-finding performance, and shared/shoulder-mounted camera
  views increase social co-presence, then closes by flagging open gaps (haptic/olfactory
  cues, EEG interfaces, avatar embodiment, real-time 3D reconstruction) as future
  directions.
