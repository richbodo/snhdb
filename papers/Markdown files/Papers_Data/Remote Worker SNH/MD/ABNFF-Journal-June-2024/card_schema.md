gist: >
  This is the inaugural June 2024 issue of The ABNFF Journal (Association of Black Nursing
  Faculty Foundation), containing six peer-reviewed articles on nursing education and
  practice affecting Black communities. Its content most relevant to SNH is a collaborative
  autoethnography in which 10 Black nursing faculty found that 'culture of oppression'
  dynamics -- hyper-scrutiny, a culture of silence, tokenism, and a 'pet-to-threat'
  withdrawal of supervisor and peer support after achieving milestones -- drove felt
  isolation, while affinity mentoring and self-built peer networks ('creating my own
  village') restored belonging and resilience. A companion qualitative study in the same
  issue found that pandemic-driven social isolation (curtailed family visits, church
  attendance, and community involvement) contributed to anxiety and depression among 30
  African-American women managing hypertension during COVID-19.
